About: GHK Voltage — 2 Ion (K⁺, Na⁺)
The 2-ion Goldman-Hodgkin-Katz equation predicts the membrane potential (Vm) from K⁺ and Na⁺ concentrations and relative permeabilities, assuming constant electric field across the membrane (Goldman 1943; Hodgkin & Katz 1949). At rest, P_K ≫ P_Na so Vm approaches E_K. During the action potential peak, Na⁺ channels open and P_Na dominates, driving Vm toward E_Na.
Vm = (RT/F) · ln[ (P_K · [K⁺]ₒ + P_Na · [Na⁺]ₒ) / (P_K · [K⁺]ᵢ + P_Na · [Na⁺]ᵢ) ] × 1000 [mV]

About: GHK Voltage — 3 Ion (K⁺, Na⁺, Cl⁻)
Extends the GHK equation with chloride (Cl⁻, z = −1). Because Cl⁻ is an anion, its inside and outside concentrations are swapped in the logarithm. At physiological values (P_Cl ≈ 0.45), Cl⁻ typically stabilizes Vm near E_K. Inhibitory GABA-A receptors increase P_Cl, pulling Vm toward E_Cl (hyperpolarization or shunting inhibition).
Vm = (RT/F) · ln[ (P_K·[K⁺]ₒ + P_Na·[Na⁺]ₒ + P_Cl·[Cl⁻]ᵢ) / (P_K·[K⁺]ᵢ + P_Na·[Na⁺]ᵢ + P_Cl·[Cl⁻]ₒ) ] × 1000 [mV] Note: Cl⁻ terms are swapped (inside in numerator, outside in denominator) because z = −1.

About: GHK 4-Ion (K⁺, Na⁺, Cl⁻, Ca²⁺)
Adding Ca²⁺ (z=+2) makes the GHK equation implicit — Vm appears on both sides — so the reversal potential is found numerically by setting the total ionic current = 0. Physiological [Ca²⁺]ᵢ ≈ 0.0001 mM (100 nM), [Ca²⁺]ₒ ≈ 2 mM. Despite the huge gradient, low P_Ca keeps Ca²⁺ contribution small at rest.
Condition: J_K + J_Na + J_Cl + J_Ca = 0 (solved numerically for Vm) Each: Jᵢ = Pᵢ·z²·F²·Vm/RT · (cᵢ − cₒ·exp(−zFVm/RT)) / (1−exp(−zFVm/RT))

About: Nernst Companion
The Nernst equation gives the equilibrium potential for each ion individually. The GHK voltage is a permeability-weighted average lying between the Nernst potentials. Driving force = Vm − E_ion: positive means ion flows outward, negative means inward.

About: GHK Current Density
GHK current density per ion: J = P·z²·F²·Vm/RT·(cᵢ−cₒ·exp(−zFVm/RT))/(1−exp(−zFVm/RT)). Units depend on P units; using relative permeabilities gives dimensionless relative current. At the reversal potential the total current = 0. The I-V curve shows each ion's current from −100 to +100 mV.
